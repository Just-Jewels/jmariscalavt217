History & About

The city of Uyuni was founded on July 11, 1889, by the constitutional president of Bolivia, Dr. Aniceto Arce, at kilometer 610 of the Antofagasta-Pulacayo railway, on the territory of the old posta vieja (old post office). Uyuni was created due to the urgent need for railway service in order to connect the Chilean border to the Pulacayo and Huanchaca mines — and from there to the city of Oruro.

In 1890, the formal founding of the city of Uyuni took place with authorities and the general public. From that moment, Uyuni began welcoming people from other places and developed into the friendly city it is known as today.
