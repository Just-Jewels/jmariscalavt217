History & About

The city of Uyuni was founded on July 11, 1889, by the then constitutional president of Bolivia,
Dr. Aniceto Arce, at kilometer 610 of the Antofagasta-Pulacayo railway, on the territory of the
so-called old posta vieja (old post office). Uyuni was created thanks to the urgent need for
railway service and with the firm idea of connecting the Chilean border to the Pulacayo and
Huanchaca mines, and from there continuing on to the city of Oruro.

Thus, in 1890, the formal founding of the city of Uyuni took place. It was celebrated with the
attendance of authorities and the general public. In this way, Uyuni began to welcome people
from other places and began to develop the image of a welcoming city.
