Train Cementery

The massive antique train cemetery just three kilometers outside of
Uyuni. The town was a major pit stop for trains transporting products
from coast to coast between the Pacific and Atlantic Ocean.


The railroad was built by British mining investors with construction
beginning in 1888 and ending in 1892. With the support the Bolivian
presidents leading to it’s eventual completion.

But the railroad was met with protest from the local indigenous
population who saw it as an intrusion of there lives. So when the
mining industry fell apart in the 1940’s the trains were abandoned
around Uyuni as a remnant of days past. They were abandoned and
became remnants of days pasted, a time of locomotives, steam engines
and the lack of stability Bolivia has faced economically.
